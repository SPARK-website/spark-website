FIRST AID

Treating cuts, scrapes, and bruises:

  • If you get a cut or a scrape, wash it immediately to prevent infection.
  • Stop the bleeding (if there is any) by pressing a clean cloth, tissue, or cotton swab firmly on the area.
  • Apply antiseptic or ointment (like Dettol, Savlon, or similar) if the wound is bigger or looks dirty.
  • Cover with a band-aid or clean dressing if it’s in an open area (like arms, legs, or hands) to keep germs out.
  • See a doctor if the wound is deep, won’t stop bleeding, or was caused by a rusty object.
  • If you are bruised, rest the area and don't bump it into anything.
  • Apply something cold to the area, such as an ice pack, for 10-15 minutes to reduce swelling.
  • Keep it raised if possible.
  • Avoid massaging the bruise.

Treating fractures, burns, and sprains:

  • For fractures, do not move the injured part.
  • If possible, support the broken part with a splint wrapped in soft cloth.
  • Apply ice to reduce swelling.
  • Get medical help ASAP.
  • If sprained, then use R.I.C.E.
  • Rest the joint.
  • Ice it for 10-15 minutes.
  • Compress it with a soft cloth, but not too tightly.
  • Elevate the joint (only if possible).
  • Call for medical help.
  • For burns, run it under cool water for 10-15 minutes.
  • Cover it using a clean cloth or gauze.
  • Don't pop blisters or apply ice, butter, or toothpaste.
  • Seek help if the burn is large, deep, or on sensitive areas like the face.

Helping someone who is choking or has fainted:

  • If the person is choking and cannot speak or cough, act immediately.
  • Give 5 firm slaps between the shoulder blades.
  • Do the Heimlich Maneuver (stand behind them, with fist above belly button, and push inwards and outwards and repeat till object is out).
  • If they collapse or don't recover, call emergency.
  • If someone has fainted, lay them on their back.
  • Raise their legs slightly and loosen tight clothing.
  • If not awake in 2-3 minutes, call for medical help.
