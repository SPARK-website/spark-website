FIRE SAFETY

Basic tips on what to do in case of a fire:

  • Stay calm and alert if there is a fire.
  • Always be aware of exits in your house, school, or public places.
  • If your clothes catch on fire, stop, drop to the ground, cover your face, and roll until the fire goes out.
  • Know the basics of using a fire extinguisher. If you don't, remember PASS (Pull pin, Aim at base, Squeeze, Sweep).
  • Dial your local emergency number immediately.
  • For extra safety, keep a small fire escape plan in your room so you always know where to go.

How to use a fire extinguisher:

  • Just remember the acronym PASS.
  • Pull the pin to discharge the extinguisher.
  • Aim at the base of the fire instead of directly at the fire to extinguish it more effectively.
  • Squeeze the top handle or lever. This releases the extinguishing agent.
  • Sweep from side to side until the fire is out.
